The government could have done more to prevent similar incidents from occurring in the future.
One of these is tying direct financial benefits to improving cyber security. Despite CISA verification, the cost of implanting modern IT security protocols
and standards can be costly. Thus, instead of expecting companies to implement IT security standards they could financially incentivize them by maybe
offering financial instances in implementing security policies or rewarding them with incentives when certain levels are reached.
In addition, since the security risk that was exploited was a data leak where the employee used the same password, the government could require
multifactor authentication, the use of multiple security keys, and complex strong passwords when handing out government tenders to organizations.
At an enterprise level, various security policies could have been implemented by Colonial Pipeline company to prevent such an attack.
For starters, they could have required all employees to have 2-factor authentication when signing. In addition, when making large changes to the
financial system or operations, they could require one more security key, thus resulting in at least two users being compromised for large damage to be/div>
done. However, by having regular education seminars on data leaks and cyber security threats for government subsidiaries and private companies, risk can be mitigated as well.
