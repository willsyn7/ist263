Colonial Pipeline Attack

One of the most prominent cyber-attacks on American infrastructure, was the colonial pipeline cyberattack. This cyberattack occurred on May 7th, 2021, and had a severe impact on private and public cybersecurity policy, IT related legislation and foreign policy. It was part of a large string of cyberattacks that targeted key infrastructure companies in the United States. The colonial Pipeline is a gas and fuel pipeline that starts in Texas and ends on the East Coast in New Jersey. It supplied Jet fuel and gasoline to the East Coast and part of the Mid East. The pipeline was constructed in 1964 and is operated by Colonial Pipeline LLC which is owned by multiple trading partners.

10 Facts about the Colonial Pipeline LLC

1. The Pipeline is 5500 mil(8850km) long.

2. It can carry up to 3 million barrels per day.

3. Originated in Huston, Texas and ends at the Port of New and New Jersey

4. The Pipeline goes though Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, Georgia, South Carolina, North Carolina, Virginia, Maryland, Delaware, Pennsylvania, and New Jersey

5. The main pipeline is 40 inches (1,000 mm) and 36 inches (910 mm) in (inner) diameter, while different pipes carry out jet fuel, diesel fuel and heating oil.

6. The Pipeline connects to major airports along the route.

7. It takes 14-24 days for products to get from Texas to New York/New Jersey

8. Operations is divided into three districts Gulf Coast District, Northeast District, Southeast District

9. EPA filed a complaint in 2000 under the Clean Water Act against the Colonial Pipeline

10. Colonial Pipeline transports 15-20 diverse types of products daily, some of which can’t be mixed together.

The Attack

The cyber-attack was conducted by the East European hacker group Dark Side. They injected ransomware into Colonial Pipeline Systems by targeting vulnerabilities, which resulted in ransomware being injected into company billing systems. Due to the ceasing of billing operations, Colonial Pipeline Company halted operations which had significant impact on East Coast and Central Jet fuel and gasoline supply. This resulted in the price of transportation and other goods increasing due to a shortage in fuel. As oil is a necessary good, it meant that prices for most commodities and resources increased as well in the short run, as companies would offload the cost to the consumer.

The attack lasted for 12 days where Darkside asked for $4.4 million in bitcoin to restore operations. After having their systems compromised, Colonial Pipeline Company contacted multiple government agencies and the cybersecurity firm Monarch. In addition, due to the impact of the attack, Biden declared a state of emergency where various government agencies got involved.

Owners

Colonial Pipeline's owners are:

• Koch Industries (a.k.a. Koch Capital Investments Company LLC, 28.09% stake ownership)

• South Korea's National Pension Service and Kohlberg Kravis Roberts (a.k.a. Keats Pipeline Investors LP, 23.44% stake ownership)

• Caisse de dépôt et placement du Québec (16.55% stake ownership via CDPQ Colonial Partners LP, acquired in 2011)

• Royal Dutch Shell (a.k.a. Shell Pipeline Company LP, 16.12% stake ownership)

• IFM Investors (a.k.a. IFM (US) Colonial Pipeline 2 LLC, 15.80% stake ownership, acquired in 2007)
