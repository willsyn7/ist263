Vulnerabilities
The vulnerability that was exploited originated from an employee using the same password on multiple accounts which was part of a data leak. As the employee had access to the billing systems and there was a lack of two factor authorization in company systems it was an easy target to exploit.


Follow up
Various government organizations and the cyber security firm Mandate was brought in as consultants and subject matter experts to restore access to company billing systems. However, as it would take more than two weeks to restore access to vital East Coast infrastructure, the ransom was paid. The FBI was able to recover 63 bitcoins from the cyberattack as an additional measure after the attack.

Impact


The ransomware attack had a long-lasting impact on US cybersecurity legislation and the cybersecurity standards that business is required to meet.

CISA

  • Lead to the CISA changing strategies to protect U.S infrastructure.
  • Provided cybersecurity information and consultation to business and organizations that are part of U.S infrastructure.
  • Lead to the creation of the CISA’s shield’s up campaign where the CISA partnered with TSA to protect US infrastructure.
  • Developed cybersecurity in responses which added more visibility to cyber threats.
  • Tried to hold executives accountable for not taking proper cybersecurity risks.

JCDC

  • Formed as a direct result and to solve the cyberattack.
  • Provides nationwide coordination against ransomware attacks.
  • Was formed as a collaboration between different government agencies to prevent ransomware attacks.

Cyber Incident Reporting for Critical Infrastructure Act of 2022 (CIRCIA)

  • Legally requires organizations to report cybersecurity incidents and ransomware payments to the government.
  • Added agility to resource deployment to manage cyber security incidents.
